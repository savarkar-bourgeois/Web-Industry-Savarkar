OUR GOAL

Web developers create and maintain websites. They are also responsible for the site’s technical aspects, such as its performance and capacity, which are measures of a website’s speed and how much traffic the site can handle.

Some developers and designers handle all aspects of a website’s construction, and others specialize in a certain aspect of it.


In addition, web developers may create content for the site. Digital designers develop, create, and test website or interface layout, functions, and navigation for usability. They are responsible for the look and functionality of the website or interface.

When creating a website, developers and designers have to make their client’s vision a reality. They build particular types of websites, such as ecommerce, news, or gaming sites, to fit clients’ needs. Different types of websites require different applications. For example, a gaming site should be able to handle advanced graphics, whereas an ecommerce site would need a payment-processing application. The developer decides which applications and designs will best fit the site, and the designer focuses on the look and usability of these elements across browsers or devices.

Description

Interface Designers focus on the user’s visual experience and what they see on the website. They design the screens through which the users will move and create new visual elements to keep the user hooked on the page. They use different interactive properties to achieve this.

Their job is to make sure that every page and step a user will experience in their interaction with the website, will lead to the vision of UX designers.

Skills & Education Required

Entry-level education requirements include: Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Web Development, or Graphic Design.

Interface designers must be able to collaborate, communicate, be curious, ask questions and show user empathy.

Statistics

The average salary is $77,348 (Minimum $44,000 and Maximum $120,000)

Interface Designers salaries depend on the company they work at and their overall experience level. They are expected to experience 8% job growth from 2019-2029.

UI Designers are always in demand and there are 8,000-24,000 job openings in this field worldwide today.

Graphic Designers

Graphic Designers create visual communications to convey messages in an effective and aesthetically pleasing manner.

A Bachelor's degree from a university or private design school is essential.

Special Effects Artists

They create mechanical, optical and computer-generated effects used in video games, tv shows, music videos and movies.

No minimum educational requirements, but most have an associate’s or bachelor’s degree in visual design, animation, or art.

Median pay: $53,485

Animators

Artists who use sequential images of drawings, puppets, and models to produce commercials, computer games, music videos and websites.

Bachelor’s degree in animation, computer graphics or fine arts is essential.

Median pay: $70,530
